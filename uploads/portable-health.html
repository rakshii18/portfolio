Portable Health: making medical records effortless

At every new care touchpoint, patients recite their medical history like a rehearsed poem - dates, medications, tests, from memory, under stress. I designed a mobile experience that stores and transfers patient information, so the story is told once - and heard.

Role
Product Designer - research, prototyping, testing
Tools
InVision Studio · Adobe XD
Context
Conceptual project - improving the patient experience

The problemTelling your story like a poem

Health information lives scattered across clinics, hospitals, and paper files - so the burden of carrying it falls on the patient. Every new doctor, every visit: the same recitation of conditions, medications, and test results, performed from memory at the exact moment a person is least equipped to remember.

The friction isn't just inconvenient. Details get lost, tests get repeated, and the appointment's limited time goes to reciting the past instead of discussing the present.

ResearchListening before designing

I conducted primary research through stakeholder interviews to understand pain points across the patient journey - where information gets requested, where it gets lost, and what patients and providers each actually need at the moment of care.

The insight that shaped everything: patients don't want to manage a medical database. They want to walk into a room and be understood. The design had to do the remembering for them.

The designYour history, in your pocket

The high-fidelity prototype - built in InVision Studio and Adobe XD - centers on two views. A dashboard surfaces what matters right now: vitals, upcoming and previous visits, ongoing and discontinued medications, and the last visit's summary. A medical history view organizes every visit chronologically, with each report - detailed notes, ECG, blood work - one tap away and ready to share with a new provider.

Design decisions were validated through A/B testing with stakeholders, comparing prototype variations to settle questions with evidence rather than opinion.

ReflectionThe thread through my work

This project crystallized the belief that runs through everything I design: people shouldn't have to work to be understood by their tools. It's the same principle behind my later professional work in clinical training - reduce the friction, and what's left is the human part.
